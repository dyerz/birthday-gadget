First-time Setup

  • Step 1:
    Go to http://docs.google.com to create your own birthday spreadsheet.
  • Step 2:
    Create a new Spreadsheet by clicking "New" then "Spreadsheet".
  • Step 3:
    Save the Spreadsheet by clicking "Save" in the upper right of the window. (Note: You may choose any name for this spreadsheet.)
  • Step 4:
    Enter your birthdays into the spreadsheet.
    The "Date" of the birthday goes in the column labed "A". The person's "Name" goes in the column labed "B".
    Your birthday list is automatically saved as you type!
  • Step 5:
    Copy the entire contents of your address bar. You should see something like: "http://spreadsheets.google.com/ccc?key=##########" (Where the #'s represent the unique ID of your spreadsheet.)
    Internet Explorer:
    Firefox:
  • Step 6:
    Paste the address in the gadget. (Note: You can edit this later in the gadget Configuration.)
  • Step 7:
    Click "Save" in the gadget, and it will display the birthdays based on your current gadget settings. (To edit those settings, click on the down arrow at the top right hand corner of your gadget.)

Please send me an email if you have any trouble getting the gadget to work correctly.

Upgrade from the old Birthday Reminders Gadget

  • Step 1-7:
    Complete the steps shown above.

  • Step 8:
    Click "Convert" in the Toolbar
  • Step 9:
    Click inside the box, and press Ctrl+A to select all the text (Note: If you have a long list, be sure to scroll down to be sure you have all your birthdays.)
  • Step 10:
    Open your spreadsheet, and paste the birthdays in Column "A"
  • Step 11:
    The Google spreadsheet is automatically saved.
  • Step 12:
    Click "Clear Old Data" in the gadget. The gadget will delete the old data, and display the new data from the Google spreadsheet

Please send me an email if you have any trouble getting the convertion to work correctly.

Birthday Reminder Lite!

Using Birthday Reminder Lite, you can use the same gadget, but without the spreadsheet!

If you want to start using Birthday Reminder Lite, simply delete your spreadsheet URL in your configuration. The gadget will reload with the option to setup the Lite version.

Please send me an email if you have any trouble using Birthdays Lite.

Hidden Toolbar

The toolbar hides itself when you are not using it! Allowing you to have a nicer view of your birthdays.

If you don't see the toolbar, slowly move your mouse toward the top of the gadget from birthday list. As you approach the top, the toolbar will pop out.

Please send me an email if you have any trouble finding the toolbar.

Dragging

To allow you to futher customize your gadget, you can now re-order the columns! Just click and drag the heading of any column onto the heading of any other column.

Please send me an email if you have any trouble dragging the columns.

Access Denied, What do I do?

From time to time, you need to login to your iGoogle account. To do this, simply Click Here (http://docs.google.com).

Or, you can follow the instructions to use Birthday Reminder Lite.

Please send me an email if you have any trouble logging back in to your spreadsheet.

Other questions about the gadget

What happened to the Birthday Gadget?

One of the biggest complaints I received about the previous version of the gadget was the limit on the number of birthdays stored in the gadget. So, in order to allow you to save more birthdays than before, and to keep your birthday data secure, your birthday information will be stored in Google documents. Using Google documents allows you to save as many birthdays as you want, while keeping the information safe within your Google account. An added benefit of this method is that you can now share birthday lists. See the Google documents help files for directions on how to share your documents.

What does 'MMM DD', 'DD MMM', 'MM/DD', and 'DD/MM' mean?

The 'M' stands for Month and the 'D' stands for Day. This option allows you to choose how the Birthday Gadget displays dates. The three M's in a row represent the 3-letter abbreviation for the month. The two M's in a row represent the number equivalent of the month (1-12).
